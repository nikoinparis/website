Who I Am

I'm a Data Science major at UC Berkeley in the Applied Mathematics domain with a Statistics minor. My coursework and projects have focused on probability, time series, stochastics, numerical analysis, optimization, and data-driven problem solving.

I currently work as a Research Data Analyst at Berkeley Haas, where I conduct longitudinal time-series analysis, build automated Python data pipelines, and create executive-facing forecasts and dashboards from ranking and research data.

I also built a Tactical ETF Portfolio Research & Optimization System focused on momentum signals, trend filters, portfolio weighting, risk overlays, and benchmark evaluation.

In that project, I evaluated shared-window and ETF-specific momentum, 200-day moving average filters, inverse-volatility versus minimum-variance weighting, volatility targeting, and defensive sleeves. The final strategy achieved a 1.18 Sharpe ratio with -20.6% max drawdown and was deployed in a Streamlit dashboard for strategy selection and diagnostics.

I'm interested in quant research, data science, and machine learning roles where I can contribute strong analytical thinking, statistical modeling, and research-oriented programming.
